### 1. Cosmology & Dark Sector

* 
**Dark Matter "Soft" Scattering:** Unlike point-like WIMPs, Dark Matter is predicted to be an extended topological soliton (radius $\sim10^{-18}$ m). Direct detection experiments (e.g., LZ, XENONnT) will see an **exponential cutoff** at high recoil energies rather than a standard "hard" contact interaction.


* 
**Dark Energy "Phantom Crossing":** The equation of state $w(z)$ is predicted to cross into the **phantom regime ($w < -1$)** at late times ($z \le 0.5$). This would falsify the constant Cosmological Constant ($w = -1$).


* 
**Cored Galactic Halos:** Small-scale Dark Matter halos will exhibit **flat, constant-density cores** rather than the central "cusps" predicted by collisionless cold dark matter models, due to the geometric self-interaction of solitons.



### 2. Particle Physics & Stability

* 
**Absolute Proton Stability:** Because the gauge group is restricted by the R9 octonionic limit, there are no larger groups (like $SU(5)$ or $SO(10)$) to mediate decay. Hyper-Kamiokande is predicted to find **zero proton decay events**.


* 
**Dirac Nature of Neutrinos:** Neutrinos are predicted to be **Dirac particles**, not Majorana. Consequently, **neutrinoless double beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$)** will not be observed by experiments like LEGEND-200 or nEXO.


* 
**The "Empty Desert":** No new particles—including supersymmetric partners (gluinos, squarks), leptoquarks, or extra dimensions—will be discovered at the LHC or Future Circular Collider (FCC) up to the Planck scale.


* 
**Higgs/Top Metastability:** The universe is predicted to exist at a **metastable critical point**, a state already supported by the current measured masses of the Higgs boson ($125$ GeV) and Top quark ($173$ GeV).



### 3. Gravity & Spacetime

* 
**No Lorentz Violation:** Spacetime will preserve **Lorentz invariance** strictly to the Planck scale. No dispersion in gamma-ray bursts or other indicators of broken Lorentz symmetry will be found.


* 
**Stochastic Gravitational Wave Background:** A specific background signal peaking in the **millihertz range** is predicted, resulting from the topological "freeze-out" of the R11/R12 geometric transition, detectable by **LISA**.


* 
**Asymptotic Safety:** Gravity is predicted to be **non-perturbatively renormalizable**, showing no semiclassical breakdown at the Planck scale but rather a transition to a scale-invariant fixed point.
